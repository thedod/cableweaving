Cable Weaving

Stories woven out of cables

And Sharkey says: "Hey sport. You connect the dots. You pick up the pieces."
Questions? Join our chat room (courtesy of Wikileaks Press).

CableWeaver lets you see CableGate references between cables in context, and select cables that relate to the same story - thus creating story lines. These story lines can be exported as skeleton web pages you can edit.
All story-line pages on this site were initially exported from CableWeaver. Quotes, remarks, etc. were then added by me and others.

Contributing to this site:

CableWeaving story-line pages are stored at codepen.io. They all have an Edit this Pen link (bottom left) that lets you view and edit their HTML. If you click on fork, you'll get your own copy of that page that you can edit and save. If you think your version has something interesting to offer, please leave a message at the original pen's chat (see example). You may also decide to use CableWeaver yourself, and generate your own story-lines (using the export button). If you want to contribute a story line here, simply create a new pen at codepen.io, paste the HTML there, and tell me about it.

Note: Although it's not mandatory, it is advised to be logged in to codepen.io while creating your own content, so that you can don't lose the ability to edit it later on. Also - if you're not logged in, you can't leave comments at codepen. If it's important for you to stay anonymous, you can tell me about your fork via a secure form instead.
